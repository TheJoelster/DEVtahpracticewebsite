So what are quadratics?

Quadratic functions have a degree of 2, meaning one term with x in it will be raised to the power of two. Displayed graphically, this forms a bowl shape. There are many ways to write a quadratic function, but for the purpose of this lesson, we are going to use something called standard form, which is written as: \[y = ax^2 + bx + c\]

The main idea of our functions is that when we plug in a value for x, we get the corresponding y value. All the other numbers (a, b, and c) will modify the equation to make it look like a parabola. To find specific points along our graph, we can plug in either x or y, and we'll get the corresponding y or x value.
